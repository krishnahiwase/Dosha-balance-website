About Ayurveda

Ayurveda is a 5,000-year-old system of natural healing that originated in India. The term Ayurveda combines two Sanskrit words: "ayur" meaning life and "veda" meaning knowledge or science.

According to Ayurveda, each person has a unique constitution (Prakriti) determined by the balance of three energies or doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha. These doshas govern all physical and mental processes and provide a framework for understanding how to maintain health and treat imbalances.

By understanding your dominant dosha, you can make lifestyle and dietary choices that promote balance and well-being.

Vata

Air & Space

Characteristics:

  • Creative, quick-thinking, and energetic
  • Thin, light body frame
  • Dry skin and hair
  • Irregular appetite and digestion
  • Tendency toward anxiety and restlessness

Balancing Tips:

  • Maintain regular routines
  • Stay warm and grounded
  • Practice calming activities
  • Eat warm, nourishing foods
  • Get adequate rest

Pitta

Fire & Water

Characteristics:

  • Intelligent, focused, and ambitious
  • Medium build with good muscle tone
  • Warm skin and sharp features
  • Strong appetite and digestion
  • Tendency toward irritability when stressed

Balancing Tips:

  • Stay cool and avoid overheating
  • Practice moderation in all activities
  • Engage in calming recreation
  • Eat cooling, fresh foods
  • Avoid excessive competition

Kapha

Earth & Water

Characteristics:

  • Calm, nurturing, and patient
  • Solid, heavier body frame
  • Smooth, oily skin and thick hair
  • Steady appetite and slow digestion
  • Tendency toward attachment and complacency

Balancing Tips:

  • Stay active and stimulated
  • Embrace change and new experiences
  • Practice energizing activities
  • Eat light, warming foods
  • Avoid excessive sleep
